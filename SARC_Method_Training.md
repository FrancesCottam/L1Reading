### SLIDE 2: WHAT IS SARC?
**Title:** The SARC Method Explained

**Content:**
SARC is your 4-step strategy for every text in the exam:

**S** - Survey (Skim)
**A** - Analyze  
**R** - Reference (Scan)
**C** - Check

**Why use SARC?**
- Saves time in the exam
- Keeps you focused
- Ensures you find the right answers
- Works for ALL text types

**Interactive element:** "Click the arrow to learn each step ‚Üí"

---

### SLIDE 3: S - SURVEY (SKIM)
**Title:** Step 1: Survey - Get the Big Picture

**What to do:**
- Quickly look at the title, headings, images
- Read the first sentence of each paragraph
- Notice any bold text, bullet points, subheadings
- Get a sense of what the text is about

**Why?**
To understand the layout and general meaning WITHOUT wasting time reading every word. Think of it like looking at a map before starting a journey.

**Time:** 30-60 seconds

**Interactive element:** 
"Try it! Look at the text on the next slide and survey it. Time yourself for 45 seconds."

### SLIDE 6: A - ANALYZE
**Title:** Step 2: Analyze the Questions

**What to do:**
- Read each question CAREFULLY
- Underline or highlight key words
- Ask yourself: "What type of question is this?"
  - Vocabulary?
  - Grammar?
  - Main idea?
  - Specific detail?

**Why?**
Understanding the question helps you know EXACTLY what to look for in the text.

**Example:**
Question: "What is the **main purpose** of this document?"
Key words: main purpose (looking for overall reason, not details)

### SLIDE 9: R - REFERENCE (SCAN)
**Title:** Step 3: Reference - Find the Answer

**What to do:**
- Go BACK to the text
- SCAN for the specific information
- Don't re-read everything - just search for key words
- Remember: The answer is ALWAYS in the text

**Scanning techniques:**
- Look for numbers, names, dates (they stand out)
- Find key words from the question
- Read just that sentence + the one before and after

**Important:** This is a READING test, not a knowledge test. You're finding evidence in the text!

### SLIDE 12: C - CHECK
**Title:** Step 4: Check Your Answer

**What to do:**
- Re-read the question
- Check your answer makes sense
- Does it answer what was actually asked?
- Is it based on the text (not your own ideas)?

**Common mistakes to avoid:**
‚ùå Choosing an answer that's true but doesn't answer the question
‚ùå Using your own knowledge instead of the text
‚ùå Not reading all the options carefully

**In the exam:**
Leave a few minutes at the end to check all your answers!

### SLIDE 18: SARC QUICK REFERENCE
**Title:** SARC Method - Your Exam Day Reminder

**For EVERY text in the exam, use SARC:**

| Step | What to do | Time |
|------|------------|------|
| **S** - Survey | Skim title, headings, structure | 30-60 sec |
| **A** - Analyze | Read questions, highlight key words | 30 sec per Q |
| **R** - Reference | Scan text for specific answers | 20-30 sec per Q |
| **C** - Check | Verify answer matches question | 10 sec per Q |

**Top Tips:**
‚úì Survey BEFORE reading questions
‚úì Analyze tells you what to look for
‚úì Reference = evidence hunting
‚úì Check prevents silly mistakes

**Remember:** You have roughly 1 minute per question. SARC keeps you on track!

### SLIDE 19: COMMON SARC MISTAKES
**Title:** Avoid These Common Mistakes

**‚ùå MISTAKE 1:** Reading the whole text carefully first
**‚úì FIX:** Survey quickly, then let questions guide your detailed reading

**‚ùå MISTAKE 2:** Answering from memory/knowledge
**‚úì FIX:** Always reference back to find evidence in the text

**‚ùå MISTAKE 3:** Spending too long on one question
**‚úì FIX:** If stuck after 1-2 minutes, make your best guess and move on

**‚ùå MISTAKE 4:** Not checking answers at the end
**‚úì FIX:** Leave 5 minutes to review all answers

**‚ùå MISTAKE 5:** Choosing an answer that's true but doesn't answer the question
**‚úì FIX:** Always check what the question is actually asking

## TEACHER NOTES FOR GOOGLE SLIDES IMPLEMENTATION

### How to Make This Interactive:

1. **Add Navigation Buttons:**
   - Insert arrow shapes on each slide
   - Link arrows to next/previous slides
   - Add "Click here" buttons that link to answer slides

2. **Hide Answer Slides:**
   - Make answer slides "skip to next slide" unless directly accessed
   - Use dark overlays with click-to-reveal functionality

3. **Add Timers:**
   - Use countdown GIFs (search "45 second timer GIF")
   - Embed YouTube timer videos

4. **Color Coding:**
   - Survey = Blue
   - Analyze = Green  
   - Reference = Orange
   - Check = Purple

5. **Interactive Elements:**
   - Text boxes students can type in
   - Checkboxes for self-assessment
   - Drag-and-drop answer options

6. **Animations:**
   - Fade in key points
   - Highlight boxes around important text
   - Appear/Disappear for answers

### For Google Classroom:
- Share as "Make a copy for each student"
- Students work through at their own pace
- They can type answers directly into slides
- Save their work and review later

### Differentiation:
- **Support:** Provide sentence starters, pre-highlighted key words
- **Challenge:** Add extension questions, timed challenges
- **EAL Support:** Include visual cues, glossary slide
